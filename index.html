Who We Are

A club built on brotherhood, purpose, and service.

Founded in October 2013, House 136 Club is a registered non-profit organisation operating under the laws of Nigeria. What started as a bond between friends has grown into a structured movement — united by one shared conviction:

"To provide humanitarian services, redevelop communities, and enhance the quality of life for our members and the community at large."

Twice every year, through our flagship Help the Needy events, we mobilise our resources and take direct action — visiting hospitals, supporting sickle cell centers, and distributing financial aid to those who need it most.

Our work is not fundraising. It is fellowship in action.

Our Mission

To promote good community relations — economically, socially, and educationally — while providing humanitarian services that redevelop communities and enhance the quality of life for members and the public.

Our Vision

A Nigeria where no member of our community is left without support. Where brotherhood transcends tribe, and collective action creates lasting change for the less privileged.

Our Work

Where we've shown up.

Every location below is a story of real people helped, real bills paid, and real hope delivered.

Juvenile Welfare Center

Juvenile Welfare Center

We visited the Juvenile Welfare Center and met close to fifty children, many of them abandoned or displaced, with nowhere else to turn. House 136 provided food supplies and financial support, standing with them through a season that has been hard for everyone.

2018
Hospital Outreach

Military Hospital Yaba

At Military Hospital Yaba, our mission was simple: touch as many lives as possible. We encountered patients facing circumstances no one deserves, offering words of encouragement alongside direct financial support — for some, that meant settling their hospital bills in full.

2022
Sickle Cell Center

Sickle Cell Foundation Nigeria

Our visit to the Sickle Cell Foundation Nigeria carried one purpose: to reach as many lives as we could. We met individuals facing battles far beyond what anyone should bear, and we responded with words of genuine encouragement and meaningful financial assistance.
